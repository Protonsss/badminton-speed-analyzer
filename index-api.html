How to Film for Best Accuracy

  • Camera Position: Place camera perpendicular to the court side, elevated 2-3 meters
  • Frame Rate: Use 60fps minimum, 120fps or 240fps for best results
  • Lighting: Ensure even lighting, avoid shadows on court
  • Court Visibility: All 4 corners of the court must be clearly visible
  • Focus: Keep camera steady, use a tripod if possible
  • Shuttlecock: Use a white shuttle for best contrast

How It Works

Calibration

Mark 4 corners of the court to map video pixels to real-world meters using homography transformation.

Detection & Tracking

PyTorch YOLOv8 detects the shuttlecock, then optical flow + Kalman filtering track it frame-by-frame.

Speed Calculation

Compute speeds from metric positions. Savitzky-Golay smoothing removes noise. Physics-based quadratic drag fit estimates initial smash speed.

Accuracy

With proper filming (60+ fps, good lighting, perpendicular angle), accuracy is typically within 3-5 km/h.
